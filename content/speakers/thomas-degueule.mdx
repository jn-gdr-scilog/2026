## Biographie

I am a CNRS researcher in the Software Engineering group at LaBRI (Bordeaux, France). My research revolves around software and software languages, with a focus on evolution, modularity, and comprehension. I craft tools to help researchers analyze software and assist developers in evolving their codebases. More details in my CV.

My main research interests include:

- Software evolution
- Software libraries, APIs, ecosystems, and dependencies
- Empirical software engineering
- Software language engineering and domain-specific languages

In past lives, I spent three years as a postdoctoral researcher with the Software Analysis and Transformation group at CWI (Amsterdam, Netherlands) and completed my PhD as part of the DiverSE group at IRISA (Rennes, France).
